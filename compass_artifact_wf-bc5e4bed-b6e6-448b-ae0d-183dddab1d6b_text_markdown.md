# Georgia Feasibility-Study Research Desk: The Oversupply & Pipeline Monitor

*Data current to Q2 2026. Every figure carries a named source and date. Claims that cannot be tied to a named source are flagged with "FLAG:" rather than asserted. Real-estate metrics are point-in-time and vary by data vendor; ranges are presented where vendors diverge.*

## TL;DR

- **A single statewide Georgia feasibility assumption is indefensible in 2026 because the state is three unrelated economies wearing one flag.** Metro Atlanta (~6.4M) is a Sun Belt digestion story that overbuilt multifamily, self-storage, and big-box industrial in 2021–2024 and is only now clearing the glut; coastal Savannah is a port-and-logistics megamarket absorbing enormous speculative industrial supply while the $12.6B Hyundai Metaplant reshapes its labor and housing demand; and rural south/central Georgia is a flat-to-shrinking agricultural base (Albany, Mitchell County, Clayton County all lost population 2020–2024, per U.S. Census Vintage 2024). The same asset class reads oppositely across these three zones — industrial vacancy sat near **4% in Atlanta's tightest infill** yet **10.8% in Savannah at year-end 2025** (Cushman & Wakefield MarketBeat / team912 citing C&W, Q4 2025).
- **The signature Atlanta story is oversupply-turned-digestion, not collapse.** Metro Atlanta multifamily vacancy spiked to ~11.1% with tumbling rents after a record ~21,200 units delivered in 2023 (Multifamily Acquisition Advisors, 2025), then compressed to roughly 5.9%–6.1% by year-end 2025 as deliveries fell >50% from the Q3 2024 peak (Northmarq, Q4 2025); per Marcus & Millichap's 2026 Atlanta Multifamily Investment Forecast, vacancy is projected to compress 50 bps to 5.2% and average rent to "increase 4.1% to $1,650 per month," ranking Atlanta #2 nationally for rent growth — the exact 2021-vintage assumptions underwriters must NOT re-import at peak. Self-storage remains genuinely oversupplied: metro Atlanta "packed on more new supply of self-storage space in 2025—in excess of 2.2 million square feet—than all other U.S. markets," ~200,000 sf more than #2 Phoenix (StorageCafe/Yardi Matrix via Urbanize Atlanta, Dec 2025), and posted ~-8% YoY street-rate declines.
- **Georgia is a FULL CON state IN TRANSITION — the single most valuable competitor-error correction.** Georgia retains Certificate of Need (O.C.G.A. Title 31, Ch. 6; administered by the Department of Community Health, Office of Health Planning), but HB 1339 (signed April 19, 2024; effective July 1, 2024) enacted the deepest reform in decades — new exemptions for birthing centers, certain psychiatric/substance-abuse programs, rural hospitals, and expanded ASC/imaging carve-outs — while KEEPING CON for new hospitals and nursing-home/skilled-nursing beds. As one health-law analysis put it, "CON Law remains alive in Georgia" (Maynard Nexsen, Aug 2024). The feasibility implication: healthcare oversupply risk is RISING for deregulated services and still allocation-gated for beds. Competitors citing "Georgia requires CON for everything" are now wrong for the HB 1339-deregulated services.
- **The competitor-absent deliverable is a metro×asset Oversupply & Pipeline Monitor paired with an SBA/USDA funding-routing map keyed to the Atlanta-vs-Savannah-vs-rural split.** Layered on top: the data-center land rush (per CBRE's North America Data Center Trends H2 2025, "Atlanta ended 2025 with 1,459.2 megawatts (MW) of total inventory, up 458.8 MW year-over-year, making it the second-largest data center market in the country behind Northern Virginia," with 2,076 MW under construction and vacancy that "tightened to just 2%") and its Georgia Power load-growth/rate overhang; the Hurricane Helene inland+coastal storm shock (Sept 2024); and the film-industry soundstage base — the Georgia analogs to Texas ERCOT/TWIA and Florida insurance/condo.

## Key Findings

1. **Atlanta multifamily is the textbook Sun Belt digestion case — oversupplied in 2023–2024, now rebalancing fast.** Vacancy peaked near 11.1% in 2023 on a record ~21,200 deliveries; by year-end 2025 the pipeline had fallen to ~16,800 units underway (down ~50% from the Q3 2024 peak) and vacancy compressed to ~5.9%–6.1% (Northmarq, Q4 2025). Marcus & Millichap projects 5.2% vacancy and +4.1% effective rent growth ($1,650/mo) for 2026 — ranking Atlanta #2 among major U.S. metros for rent growth after two years of declines (Marcus & Millichap / Atlanta Agent Magazine / CRE Daily, Jan 2026). Underwriting either the 2023 crash OR the 2026 rebound as a static baseline is the core failure mode.

2. **Savannah is the hottest port-logistics industrial market in America AND the clearest speculative-oversupply digestion story.** The Port of Savannah handled nearly 5.7M TEUs in 2025 — up 2.6% (146,000 TEUs) over 2024, its second-busiest year ever behind 2022 — and the Georgia Ports Authority is executing a "self-financed $4.5 billion investment plan for the next ten years" (GPA press release quoting CEO Griff Lynch, Jan 27, 2026); yet ~9.8M sf of speculative deliveries in 2025 pushed industrial vacancy to 10.8% at year-end 2025 (Cushman & Wakefield MarketBeat, Q4 2025) — up from 0.8% in 2022. The digestion is real but the fundamentals are structural, anchored by the $12.6B Hyundai Metaplant.

3. **The Hyundai Metaplant + EV/battery cluster is a genuine demand engine with real timing risk.** HMGMA (Ellabell, Bryan County) is a $7.59B assembly plant ($12.6B including LG/SK battery JVs) — the largest economic-development project in Georgia history — that opened March 26, 2025 and targets 8,500 direct jobs by 2031 (Hyundai states the site's full target is "8,500 people on-site" plus "another 6,900 supplier jobs off-site"), with ~3,200 employed as of October 2025 (Hyundai/AJC/Design News, 2025). A September 4, 2025 ICE raid detained approximately 475 workers — "the largest single site enforcement operation in the history of Homeland Security Investigations," per HSI Special Agent in Charge Steven Schrank (CNN, Sept 5, 2025) — most South Korean nationals, delaying battery-plant production 2–3 months. SK Battery announced 958 job cuts at its Commerce plant in March 2026 (Partners Real Estate, Q1 2026). Sizing workforce housing or supplier absorption to peak projected employment before it materializes is dangerous.

4. **Metro Atlanta is now the #2 U.S. data-center market, and Georgia Power's load-growth response is both the biggest demand tailwind and the biggest regulatory/cost risk in the state.** Per CBRE, Atlanta ended 2025 with 1,459.2 MW of inventory (up 458.8 MW YoY), 2,076 MW under construction, and vacancy of just 2% (CBRE North America Data Center Trends H2 2025). Georgia Power's 2025 IRP projects ~8,500 MW of load growth by 2030; the PSC on Dec 19, 2025 approved 9,985 MW of new generation (~80% for data centers) with base rates frozen through 2028 (Georgia PSC / Georgia Recorder, Dec 2025). Local moratoriums are proliferating.

5. **Georgia is a FULL CON state IN TRANSITION (HB 1339, 2024) — a dynamic middle-to-full position distinct from static-full New York/Illinois, no-CON Texas/Pennsylvania, and nursing-bed-only Ohio.** CON still gates new hospitals and nursing/skilled-nursing beds; HB 1339 deregulated birthing centers, certain behavioral-health programs, and rural hospitals, and eased ASC/imaging/capital thresholds. Healthcare oversupply risk is therefore RISING for the deregulated services. This is the sharpest competitor-error correction available on the Georgia child pages.

#### Multifamily

**Atlanta — DIGESTING→BALANCED (the signature story).** Metro Atlanta was one of the most oversupplied U.S. multifamily markets of the cycle. A record ~21,200 units delivered in 2023 drove vacancy to ~11.1% and pushed rents down (Multifamily Acquisition Advisors, 2025). Digestion has been rapid: deliveries fell >50% from the Q3 2024 peak; the pipeline stood at ~16,800 units at year-end 2025 (Northmarq, Q4 2025), and permitting fell ~28% from 2024. Vacancy compressed to ~5.9%–6.1% by late 2025 (Northmarq forecast, Q4 2025). CRE Daily (Apr 2026) reported vacancy easing from 11.9% to 11.1% YoY on its inventory definition with effective rent ~$1,631 — illustrating the wide vendor spread (5.9% vs. 11.1%) that underwriters must reconcile by data source. Marcus & Millichap's 2026 forecast: vacancy to 5.2%, average rent +4.1% to $1,650/mo (#2 among major U.S. metros), ~8,400 units delivered (slowest pace in over a decade); MD John M. Leonard noted, "Even as new development slows, Atlanta's steady job growth and population gains are helping to keep apartment demand resilient." Submarket texture: intown Midtown/Buckhead/Downtown posted the strongest 2024–2025 fundamentals (fewer than 600 core units slated for 2026 delivery per Marcus & Millichap), while suburban Gwinnett/Cobb/Cumming/Alpharetta absorbed ~70% of 2025 investment volume. **Supply-pressure read: DIGESTING, approaching BALANCED. Benchmark that flips the read back to OVERSUPPLIED: a re-acceleration of deliveries above ~15,000 units/yr or vacancy backing up above ~8%.**

**Savannah — DIGESTING.** Growing on port/Hyundai in-migration but also absorbing meaningful new supply. *FLAG: metro-level Savannah multifamily vacancy/rent figures were not verified to a named vendor this cycle; pull from CoStar/Yardi before publishing.*

**Augusta — BALANCED.** Anchored by Fort Eisenhower (formerly Fort Gordon) cyber mission, medical (Wellstar MCG), and the Masters. Relatively stable; the demand base is government/military/medical rather than speculative. *FLAG: metro-level figures unverified this cycle.*

**Athens — BALANCED, student-anchored.** Driven by the University of Georgia. *FLAG: metro-level figures unverified this cycle.*

**Columbus / Macon / Albany — BALANCED→soft.** Columbus (Fort Moore, Synovus HQ, Aflac), Macon (logistics/medical), Albany (agriculture, flat-to-declining population). *FLAG: metro-level figures unverified this cycle.*

#### Self-Storage — OVERSUPPLIED (Atlanta)

Metro Atlanta "packed on more new supply of self-storage space in 2025—in excess of 2.2 million square feet—than all other U.S. markets," roughly 200,000 sf more than #2 Phoenix (StorageCafe/Yardi Matrix via Urbanize Atlanta, Dec 2025); SpareFoot/Yardi Matrix cite 2.4M sf and rank Atlanta #1 of 323 U.S. metros for new-unit construction (Jan 2026). Metro inventory now exceeds 54M sf, near 10 sf/capita (Urbanize/StorageCafe, Dec 2025) — above the national average, which StorageCafe pegs at ~7.6 sf/capita (2025). Average monthly rate ~$120 (StorageCafe, Dec 2025). Yardi Matrix's national report (Multi-Housing News, Mar 2026) explicitly names Atlanta among Sun Belt metros "struggling due to oversupply." Storage was among metros posting ~-8% YoY street-rate declines tied to oversupply (List Self Storage/StorTrack, 2025). *Note: sources conflict on Atlanta's per-capita read — StorageCafe elsewhere (Q3 2025 sales note) described Atlanta as "relatively undersupplied" at 4.6 sf/capita, which appears to use a narrower geography; the ~8–10 sf/capita metro figures are the more defensible institutional read. FLAG the discrepancy.* **Supply-pressure read: OVERSUPPLIED; the read flips toward BALANCED once trailing-12-month deliveries fall below ~1M sf and street rates stabilize YoY.**

#### Industrial / Warehouse — SIGNATURE ASSET CLASS

**Atlanta industrial — DIGESTING.** One of the largest U.S. industrial markets (I-85/I-75/I-20, Airport/South Atlanta). After a record big-box wave, 12-month net absorption turned negative for the first time since 2011 in early 2025 (Bull Realty citing CoStar, 2025). Metro vacancy rose from ~4% (2022) to a range of ~8.1%–9.0% through 2025 (Partners Real Estate/Lincoln/CoStar, 2025). Q2 2025 posted ~-2.24M sf of negative absorption driven by big-box move-outs in Airport/I-85 South (Procter & Gamble vacating ~1M sf; Lincoln Property, Q2 2025). Recovery followed: Q3 2025 net absorption +1.67M sf, Q4 2025 +3.1M sf, and Q1 2026 +4.1M sf (the strongest since Q2 2024), though metro vacancy still edged up to 8.7% on Airport/South Atlanta weakness (DHL vacating ~1M sf) (Partners Real Estate, Q1 2026). The construction pipeline fell to ~14.9–17.0M sf — a decade low — and NNN asking rents softened to ~$8.81/sf (down 6.8% YoY) in Q1 2026 (Partners, Q1 2026). I-85 North is the demand anchor; Airport/South Atlanta the drag. **Read: DIGESTING; flips to BALANCED as the sub-15M sf pipeline burns down and vacancy compresses back toward ~7%.**

**Savannah industrial — DIGESTING (structural growth).** The Port of Savannah is the largest single-terminal container facility in North America and one of the fastest-growing U.S. industrial markets. Nearly 50M sf delivered since 2020, including a record 22.2M sf in 2023 (CBRE, 2024), driving vacancy from 0.8% (2022) to 7.7% (2023) and onward. 2024 ended at 8.6% despite 11.1M sf of speculative deliveries (C&W, Q1 2025); 2025 saw ~9.8M sf more delivered and vacancy at 10.8% at year-end (C&W MarketBeat, Q4 2025). Vendor spread is wide: Colliers reported Q2 2025 vacancy peaking at 11.72% then improving to 10.25% in Q3 2025; Lee & Associates reported 13.48% in Q2 2025 on a different inventory base. Total inventory now exceeds 163M sf (Colliers, Q2 2025). Absorption remains solid (8.1M sf YTD through Q3 2025, at the long-term average — Colliers). Asking rents ~$8.42/sf (Lee & Associates, Q2 2025). The contrast with Atlanta is the flagship divergence datapoint: Savannah at ~10.8% vacancy vs. Atlanta's tightest infill near 4% (team912 citing C&W, Q4 2025). **Read: DIGESTING with structural upside; the risk is speculative starts re-accelerating ahead of absorption.**

**Megaproject demand — Hyundai Metaplant + EV/battery cluster.** HMGMA (Bryan County/Ellabell), $7.59B assembly ($12.6B with LG Energy Solution and SK On battery JVs), opened March 26, 2025; capacity ramping to 500,000 vehicles/yr with a $2.7B Phase 2 (announced Sept 18, 2025) adding 3,000 jobs; target 8,500 direct jobs by 2031; ~3,200 employed Oct 2025 (Hyundai/AJC/Design News, 2025). The co-located $4.3B Hyundai–LG HL-GA Battery JV would build 30 GWh, "enough for 300,000 EVs" (Design News/Automotive Logistics, Sept 2025). Suppliers: ~17 announced factories, ~$2.7B and ~7,000 more jobs (Wikipedia citing company data, 2025). Broader cluster: Hyundai-SK Battery plant (~3.3M sf, Kingston/Bartow, delivering Q1 2026) and Qcells solar (~500,000 sf, White/Cartersville) (Partners Real Estate, Q4 2025). **FLAG: Rivian's ~$5B East Atlanta (Social Circle/Stanton Springs) plant was paused in 2024; current status not verified to a named source this cycle — pull the latest before publishing.**

#### Office — Atlanta bifurcated (OVERSUPPLIED commodity / BALANCED trophy)

Metro Atlanta office vacancy is elevated at ~26.5%–30%+ overall (Partners Real Estate, Q3 2025; The Real Deal citing JLL cites 27% overall / 92% occupancy for 2016+ vintage, Feb 2025). The market is sharply bifurcated by "flight-to-quality": buildings built 2016–2021 average ~92% occupancy while older stock languishes. Submarket rents Q4 2025 (Cumberland & Worthy / marietta cre): Midtown $43.30/sf (32.1% vacancy, concentrated in older product), Buckhead $40.46/sf (28.8%, first time above $40, +7.5% YoY, strongest metro absorption +308,843 sf in 2025), Downtown $31.73/sf (30.2%), Cumberland/Galleria $29.56/sf (20.0%, tightest). Distress is concrete: Buckhead's Piedmont Center (14 buildings) fell from a $657M (2021) valuation to ~$200M and faced foreclosure after Ardent Companies defaulted on a $330M Morgan Stanley loan (The Real Deal, Feb 2025). Atlanta is the nation's #6 office-to-residential converter with ~2,200 units in the conversion pipeline (Urbanize citing JLL, Feb 2025). Secondary metros are thin. **Read: OVERSUPPLIED for commodity/Class B; BALANCED for trophy/new Class A.**

#### Hotels — BALANCED, with two distinctive demand spikes

Atlanta had the #2 U.S. hotel construction pipeline in Q1 2026 with 158 projects / 17,524 rooms, behind only Dallas (Lodging Econometrics, Q1 2026). National RevPAR is soft: CoStar/Tourism Economics forecast full-year 2026 U.S. RevPAR +0.6% (after -0.3% in 2025).

**Atlanta / 2026 FIFA World Cup.** Atlanta (Mercedes-Benz Stadium) hosts matches including a semifinal. CoStar/Tourism Economics project host-market RevPAR +12.7% during the June–July tournament window and +3.8% for the full year, but caution the national impact is "negligible" (Hotel Dive/Lodging Magazine, Feb 2026). By May 2026, the AJC reported Atlanta "faring better than most" host cities but that the "economic lift may fall short of expectations," with FIFA-driven room-block "wash" and softening rate discipline (AJC, May 2026; Hotel Management, 2026). **Treat the World Cup as a one-time 2026 demand pulse, not a stabilized-year assumption.**

**Augusta / the Masters.** Augusta's annual Masters Tournament (April) is a unique, singular RevPAR spike — private-jet traffic surges at Augusta Regional and ADR multiples spike for one week — but the market reverts to a military/medical base the rest of the year. Underwriting a Masters-week ADR into a stabilized-year pro forma is an Augusta-specific failure mode. *FLAG: Augusta-specific STR/CoStar RevPAR figures not verified to a named source this cycle.*

**Savannah** tourism boom continues to support lodging demand. *FLAG: Savannah RevPAR figures unverified this cycle.*

#### Niche

**(a) Data centers — MAJOR THEME.** Metro Atlanta is the #2 U.S. data-center market: per CBRE (North America Data Center Trends H2 2025), Atlanta ended 2025 with 1,459.2 MW of inventory (up 458.8 MW YoY), 2,076 MW under construction, and vacancy that "tightened to just 2%," supported by 3+ GW of long-term power commitments. Georgia Trend (Dec 2025) reported >$40B in data-center investment commitments in the first seven months of 2025 alone. The land rush is concentrated in Douglas, Henry, Fulton, Fayette (QTS's 615-acre, 13-building campus), Newton (Meta), and Bartow ("Project Bunkhouse," a proposed ~$19B / 8.7M sf, per Mordor Intelligence citing filings, 2025). Hyperscaler names: Microsoft, Google, QTS, Meta, AWS (which plans $11B more, per Georgia Trend). **Georgia Power load growth is the regulatory hinge:** the 2025 IRP projects ~8,500 MW of load growth by 2030; the PSC approved 9,985 MW of new generation on Dec 19, 2025 (~80% for data centers), largely via five new gas units, with base rates frozen through 2028 and data-center-specific rate rules to protect residential ratepayers (Georgia PSC / Georgia Recorder / Utility Dive, 2025). SELC estimates the plan could cost customers $50–60B over asset life if forecasts miss (SELC, Dec 2025 — advocacy source, flagged as such). A December 2025 audit (UGA Carl Vinson Institute) found the data-center tax exemption cost ~$474.2M in forgone FY2025 revenue and that only ~30% of data centers were attributable to the exemption (Atlanta Tech News, 2025). Local moratoriums (Coweta, DeKalb, Douglas, Troup; Atlanta BeltLine/MARTA bans; Camden County) are proliferating.

**(b) Student housing.** Anchored by UGA (Athens — large), Georgia Tech and Georgia State (Atlanta), Kennesaw State, Augusta University, SCAD (Savannah), and Mercer (Macon). Purpose-built student-housing cranes were part of Midtown's 2022–2023 boom (Multifamily Acquisition Advisors, 2025). *FLAG: enrollment-based supply metrics not verified to a named source this cycle.*

**(c) Film/entertainment real estate.** Georgia is a top U.S. film-production state ("Y'allywood"), #2 nationally in soundstage space behind only L.A. (Georgia Dept. of Economic Development, 2024). Stage space grew from 45,000 sf (2010) to >4.5M sf (2024) across Trilith (34 stages, ~1M+ sf, Fayetteville), Tyler Perry Studios (330 acres, Fort McPherson), Assembly (Doraville), Shadowbox, Blackhall, and BlueStar. The 30% transferable film tax credit ("Peach logo") drives a ~$4B+ entertainment economy (Georgia Entertainment, Dec 2025). Headwinds: post-strike slowdown, AI (Tyler Perry paused an $800M expansion in 2024), and California/European incentive competition (Variety/Georgia Trend, 2025). Soundstage demand is real but cyclically soft; underwrite against the production slowdown, not the 2021 peak.

**(d) Senior housing / assisted living.** Nursing-home/skilled-nursing bed supply remains CON-gated (see Layer C), so allocation-limited — a supply floor competitors miss. *FLAG: NIC MAP occupancy for Atlanta metro not verified to a named source this cycle; pull before publishing.*

**(e) EV/battery manufacturing real estate.** Covered under Industrial above (Hyundai Metaplant, SK Battery, LG JV, supplier parks).

**(f) Car wash.** *FLAG: No verifiable Georgia-specific car-wash supply data located this cycle.*

### LAYER B: Georgia-Specific Rejection / Failure Forensics

1. **Statewide-average error.** Applying one Georgia rent-growth/absorption/vacancy assumption across Atlanta (digesting), Savannah (structural growth + speculative glut), and rural south Georgia (flat/shrinking) guarantees a mispriced pro forma. Concrete: industrial vacancy read ~4% (Atlanta infill) vs. 10.8% (Savannah metro) at year-end 2025 (team912 citing C&W; C&W MarketBeat Q4 2025). Population: Dawson County grew 6.4% (2020–2024, #1 in the U.S. among counties ≥20,000) while Clayton County LOST 1,677 residents and Mitchell County lost 2.2% (U.S. Census Vintage 2024).

2. **Oversupply blindness (THE signature Georgia failure mode).** Underwriting 2021-era Sun Belt rent growth into Atlanta multifamily at the top of the delivery wave produced the 2023–2024 distress: vacancy ~11.1%, tumbling rents, foreclosures (e.g., Riverbend Apartments, early 2024), and a reported 35% insurance-premium jump on one Atlanta portfolio in 2024 (Partners Real Estate, 2025). Equally, underwriting 2021 Savannah industrial rent spikes as vacancy ran from 0.8% (2022) to 10.8% (2025) is the coastal analog. **The trap now cuts both ways: importing the 2026 M&M rebound (5.2% vacancy, +4.1% rents) as a stabilized assumption at peak optimism is the next mistake.**

3. **Megaproject timing/absorption error.** The Hyundai Metaplant and EV/battery cluster drive real demand, but sizing workforce housing or supplier absorption to peak projected employment (8,500 by 2031) before it materializes (~3,200 as of Oct 2025) is dangerous — underscored by the Sept 4, 2025 ICE raid (~475 detained, 2–3 month production delay) and SK Battery's 958-job cut at Commerce (Mar 2026) (Wikipedia/CNN/Partners Real Estate, 2025–2026). **FLAG: Rivian Georgia plant status unverified — its 2024 pause is the cautionary precedent.**

4. **Rural/shrinking-market capture-rate error.** Many south/central Georgia counties are flat or declining (Clayton -1,677; Mitchell -2.2%, 2020–2024, Census Vintage 2024). Assuming growth-market absorption/capture rates in Albany or rural agricultural counties overstates demand. Conversely, assuming stagnation in the Atlanta exurbs (Dawson +6.4%, Jackson +5.8%) or coastal Long County (~13% since 2020) understates it.

5. **Insurance/climate mispricing — coastal AND inland.** Hurricane Helene (Sept 26–27, 2024) crossed Georgia as a hurricane and produced catastrophic INLAND damage often overlooked in Georgia underwriting: Augusta/Richmond County damage exceeded $500M with ~82 mph gusts at Augusta Regional; 1.3M Georgia Power customers lost power (>7,000 poles replaced); Helene caused 25–37 deaths in Georgia (FOX5/Wikipedia/NHC, 2024). The state also carries coastal exposure at Savannah/Golden Isles. Property-insurance costs are rising statewide (Partners Real Estate cited a 35% Atlanta-portfolio jump, 2024). Underwrite inland wind, not just coastal surge.

6. **Georgia Power / energy / data-center dynamics.** The ~8,500 MW load-growth forecast and the Dec 19, 2025 PSC approval of 9,985 MW (~80% for data centers) is simultaneously a demand opportunity (power availability draws hyperscalers and manufacturers) and a cost/regulatory risk (five new gas units; SELC's $50–60B customer-cost estimate; base-rate freeze through 2028; proliferating local moratoriums) (Georgia PSC/Georgia Recorder/SELC/Utility Dive, 2025). Interconnection queues and the 30%-attributable tax-exemption audit finding add project-siting uncertainty.

7. **Property tax / other.** Georgia voters approved a statewide floating homestead exemption via constitutional amendment in Nov 2024 (HB 581), capping homestead assessment growth to inflation; 2026 follow-on fights (SB 33/HB 1116/SB 382) over local opt-outs and caps continued through Sine Die 2026 (Georgia Recorder/WABE/GBPI, 2026). Georgia's effective property-tax rate is moderate (~0.79%–0.92%; Fulton County median ~$4,033) (Tax Foundation/AARP, 2026). Construction cost/labor inflation and the Helene rebuild add cost pressure. *Note: the floating homestead exemption applies to homestead (owner-occupied residential), not directly to income-property underwriting, but signals the local-revenue/millage environment.*

### LAYER C: Georgia Certificate-of-Need Precision

**Georgia is a FULL CON state IN TRANSITION.** The program is administered by the **Georgia Department of Community Health (DCH), Office of Health Planning**, under **O.C.G.A. Title 31, Chapter 6**. **HB 1339** (signed by Gov. Kemp April 19, 2024; effective July 1, 2024) enacted the deepest CON reform in decades — but did NOT repeal CON. As Maynard Nexsen summarized (Aug 2024): *"While the changes are significant... these changes are less dramatic than expected. In short, CON Law remains alive in Georgia."* Implementing regulations (Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 111-2-2) were adopted Nov 14, 2024 and took effect Jan 13, 2025 (DCH).

**Still requires CON (allocation-gated → supply floor):**
- **New/expanded general (acute-care) hospitals**, with a conditional carve-out: new acute-care hospitals in RURAL counties are exempt IF, within 36 months, they attain teaching-hospital status OR an ACS trauma-center designation (Level I–IV). *(Note: GSU Law Review/Georgia Public Policy Foundation cite "30 months"; Maynard Nexsen and statute-based analyses cite "36 months" — flagged discrepancy.)*
- **Nursing home / skilled-nursing / intermediate-care / long-term-care beds** — UNCHANGED by HB 1339; need still set by a population-based bed formula (Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 111-2-2-.30). Personal care homes and home health agencies also still require CON.
- Diagnostic **imaging facilities** still require CON review (though equipment cost thresholds were eased — see below).

**Deregulated / newly exempted by HB 1339 (oversupply risk RISING):**
- Freestanding **birthing centers**
- **Psychiatric and substance-abuse inpatient programs** (for Medicaid/uninsured patients, via agreement with a nearby hospital)
- Hospital **perinatal services in rural counties**; reopening of recently-closed rural hospitals (targeting Cuthbert); a specific Morehouse School of Medicine Atlanta-hospital exemption.
- **Hospital capital expenditures**: the prior $10M threshold for clinical services was REMOVED (does not apply to adding inpatient beds).
- **Hospital bed increases**: exempt for +10 beds or +20% (whichever greater) over 3 years if ≥60% prior-year occupancy.
- **Imaging equipment**: removed the $3M acquisition cost limit for hospitals/physician practices; allowed some non-hospital acquisitions that don't create a new clinical service.
- **Single-specialty ASCs**: broadened to allow same-specialty non-owner physicians to use the ASC and to permit MSO/management arrangements.
- **Relocation radius** extended 3→5 miles; intra-system bed transfers permitted within 15 miles.
- **Process:** LOI now 25 days pre-application; 120-day review clock from submission; opposition window cut 60→30 days; appeals reduced from two administrative levels to one (hearing officer, then superior court).

**Feasibility implication.** Georgia is a **partially-gated, LOOSENING** CON environment — a DYNAMIC middle-to-full position distinct from static-full **New York/Illinois**, no-CON **Texas/Pennsylvania**, and nursing-bed-only **Ohio**. Feasibility studies must track WHICH services were deregulated (elevated new-supply risk — birthing centers, behavioral health, ASCs, imaging capacity, rural hospitals) versus which remain gated (allocation-limited supply floor — hospital beds, nursing/SNF beds). **Competitors citing "Georgia requires CON for everything" are now demonstrably wrong for the HB 1339-deregulated services.** *FLAG: no additional CON-reform bill was confirmed to have PASSED in the 2025 or 2026 sessions; HB 1339 directed DCH to submit further recommendations by Dec 1, 2024 — treat "no further CON law passed" as unconfirmed rather than affirmatively verified.*

## Caveats

- Real-estate figures are point-in-time and vary materially by data vendor. The clearest example: Atlanta multifamily vacancy is reported at ~5.9%–6.1% (Northmarq) vs. ~11.1% (CRE Daily's inventory definition) for late 2025; Savannah industrial vacancy at 10.25%–13.48% across Colliers, C&W, and Lee & Associates for 2025. Present ranges, not point estimates, on child pages.
- CDC coverage areas are self-reported; "#1/largest" claims (e.g., GCDC) are marketing statements, flagged as such.
- Hotel RevPAR figures blend aggregator (CoStar/STR/Tourism Economics) forecasts with primary market reports; World Cup and Masters spikes are event-driven pulses, not stabilized-year results.
- Forward-looking figures are projections, not results — especially the Hyundai/EV timeline and job counts (8,500 by 2031 is a target; ~3,200 realized as of Oct 2025), the Georgia Power load-growth forecast (8,500 MW is a projection the PSC's own staff disputed), Marcus & Millichap's 2026 Atlanta rent-growth call (+4.1%), and CON reform, which is still being implemented and could see further legislation.
- **Explicitly flagged as unverified this cycle:** metro-level multifamily/office/hotel figures for Savannah, Augusta, Columbus, Macon, Albany, Athens; Augusta/Savannah RevPAR; Rivian Georgia plant current status; NIC MAP Atlanta senior-housing occupancy; student-housing supply metrics; car-wash data; FY2026 USDA B&I/CF guarantee percentages and Georgia REAP obligations; exact GA-state SBA lender/CDC rankings; the 30- vs. 36-month rural-hospital CON carve-out discrepancy; whether any CON bill passed in 2025–2026.
